Case 1 – Summary

Case 1 analyses four IPv6 packet captures focusing on ICMPv6 traffic between two local hosts (fe80::2 and fe80::1). Across all captures, the traffic primarily consists of Neighbor Discovery Protocol messages and ICMPv6 Echo Request/Reply exchanges used for address resolution and connectivity testing.

In the initial stages, standard neighbor solicitation and advertisement messages establish link-layer communication between the hosts, followed by repeated ICMPv6 echo requests and replies confirming successful connectivity. In later stages of the captures, ICMPv6 echo replies from fe80::1 stop after packet 32, while occasional unsolicited Neighbor Advertisements continue. This indicates that the host remains active on the network but is no longer responding to ICMP echo requests, most likely due to ICMP filtering or configuration changes rather than failure or malicious activity. Overall, the behaviour observed across all four captures is consistent with normal IPv6 diagnostic and network discovery operations.
